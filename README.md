The following contract was written and agreed upon by Nicole Carrie Louderback, Levi Huff, Deepak Baral, Guy-Leroc Ossebi, and Jackson Pinchot. The contract provides expectations, objectives, and results for developing “The Culinary Mastery” web app. 

 

The contract is effective for all team members participating in the Senior Design Capstone class series in the Fall 2024 – Spring 2025 academic semesters.  

 

Scrum and Sprints 

 

We will be using Scrum, an Agile project management framework, to manage our development process. Scrum uses sprints, which are time-boxed periods (typically 2 weeks) where we focus on completing a set of functionalities. 

 

Meetings will be held each Monday of each week at 7:00pm EST unless stated otherwise. 

Communication Platform: Microsoft Teams 

 

Here's how we will integrate Scrum and Sprints: 

 

Project Backlog: We will create a comprehensive list of user stories and tasks that represent the entire scope of the Culinary Mastery app. 

Sprint Planning: Before each sprint, we will hold a planning meeting to select a set of user stories and tasks from the backlog that we believe can be completed within the 2-week timeframe. This becomes the sprint backlog. 

Weekly Scrum Meetings: We will hold brief daily meetings (usually 15-30 minutes) to discuss progress, perform demos, identify roadblocks, and adjust tasks as needed. 

Weekly Instructor Meetings: The professor scheduled a weekly meeting every Tuesday from 5:00pm-5:15pm to discuss progress and perform demos. 

Sprint Review: At the end of each sprint, we will demonstrate the completed functionalities to instructors and potentially sponsors. We will also discuss lessons learned and identify areas for improvement. 

Sprint Retrospective: After each sprint review, we will hold a retrospective meeting to reflect on the past sprint, identify areas for improvement in our Scrum process, and adjust for the next sprint. 

 

Senior Design Contract 

 

Project Summary 

 

Our educational web app aims to teach foundational cooking skills with the intention that at the end of each simple course, the user will be able to confidently apply that skill to a variety of other foods, not just to a particular recipe. Our web app will achieve high-quality online learning by integrating high-quality video tutorials, secure and scalable data storage, and efficient backend services. By using advanced technologies, we provide users with a smooth, reliable, and enriching educational experience. 

 

Problem Statement 

 

Research shows that dietary outcomes tend to be better for individuals who feel confident in their ability to prepare meals at home. (Utter, et al., 2018) Unfortunately, many feel intimidated by cooking for themselves. Another study of 6,638 students (Wilson, et al., 2017) reported that 62.5% felt comfortable preparing meals from basic ingredients, while 31% felt they could only put together ready-made ingredients, and the remaining 6% feeling they had limited to no cooking ability.  

 

Meal planning is often suggested as a primary way to start cooking more meals at home, but one study found that planning can feel overwhelming or even be a barrier to cooking at home. (Garvin TM, et al., 2019) When assessing the helpfulness of a web app intended to help low-income families with meal planning, some reported that they prefer to just cook with what they have at home.  

 

Additionally, many online learning platforms struggle with issues like limited scalability, (Jones, 2023) data security concerns, and poor video streaming quality. Elena Gutierrez, a content manager for a development agency specializing in online learning suggests keeping scalability in mind from the creation of your learning module system. Other resources that promise to teach basic cooking skills utilize blog-style sites. These sites tend to be limited in terms of usability due to confusing navigation, poor accessibility configuration and an over-abundance of ads including reoccurring popups that take up valuable screen space. These problems make it difficult for people to access effective online learning, especially in areas with unreliable internet access. 

 

Solution 

Research shows that people who cook at home tend to have better dietary outcomes. However, many feel intimidated. We address these challenges by offering: 

High-Quality Video Tutorials: We'll leverage YouTube's API platform for smooth playback and accessibility. 

Diverse & Accessible Content: We will create original video content to cater to different learning needs. We will also accommodate users with: 

Subtitles and Closed Captions: To ensure that users with hearing impairments can fully engage with the content. 

Audio Descriptions: For visually impaired users, we will provide audio descriptions that narrate non-visual elements of the videos. 

High-Contrast Themes: We will offer customizable themes that allow users to adjust the contrast of the app's interface, making it easier for users with visual impairments to read and navigate the content. 

Alternative Text for Images: We will provide descriptive alternative text for all images, ensuring that users with visual impairments can understand the content of the images. 

Multilingual Support: We will offer the app in multiple languages to cater to users from diverse backgrounds. 

By incorporating these accessibility features, we will ensure that the Culinary Mastery app is inclusive and accessible to users of all abilities. 

Secure and Scalable Storage: Azure DB will securely store video files. 

Scalable Backend Services: Node.js will enable efficient backend services to support app functions. 

By integrating these technologies, our app delivers high-quality content, secure data management, and efficient backend operations, enhancing the online learning experience for users worldwide.

Project Source 

Initial inspiration came from one of the group member’s younger siblings who struggled with online learning. After receiving several calls for help with understanding fundamental concepts, the need for an easy-to-access educational tool became apparent. An idea was proposed for a web app that offers clear instruction and explanation for basic learning processes and vocabulary across various subjects. Each group member contributed their own ideas, building upon the initial concept. The knowledge and expertise of each group member make this application an indispensable and high-quality educational tool. We all contributed in some way to the requirements analysis. Some of us had better skills to examine for project needs.  The team members felt their style of flexibility and communication allowed them to work well together as a group. 

 
Project Objectives/Goals 

The app aims to revolutionize the way individuals learn how to cook by providing a comprehensive and flexible learning platform designed to empower users with knowledge, promote continuous learning, enhance creativity and critical thinking, improve culinary skills, and reduce educational gaps. By equipping users with the confidence to master a wide range of culinary subjects, the app reduces reliance on fragmented online resources and encourages the habit of continuous learning through engaging content. It fosters creativity and critical thinking by teaching users the underlying cooking principles, such as flavor pairing, cooking techniques, and ingredient selection, and allowing them to experiment with different recipes and methods. Additionally, the app contributes to better performance in the kitchen by offering clear, structured, and interactive learning materials, including step-by-step video tutorials, quizzes, and practical exercises. By making high-quality learning resources accessible to all, the app helps bridge educational gaps, ensuring that everyone, regardless of their background or experience level, can benefit from effective and comprehensive culinary education. The app also includes features such as personalized learning paths, progress tracking, and community forums where users can share their experiences, ask questions, and receive feedback from peers and experts. This holistic approach not only enhances the learning experience but also builds a supportive community of aspiring chefs and cooking enthusiasts.

Project Scope 

Our team will develop a functional application that enables users to learn fundamental concepts through short, simple, asynchronous courses. The application will focus on an educational style rather than a blog style, serving as a structured educational tool. 

Courses will be organized by method, featuring step-by-step instructions, including how to prepare food and use relevant tools. Videos will cover detailed processes and techniques, while written explanations will cover vocabulary, technique, and safety information, using Artificial Intelligence. 

Courses will include fun, AI-generated interactive quizzes and personalized feedback to reinforce understanding and keep users engaged.

Technologies Used 

YouTube API: We will integrate the YouTube API to host and stream high-quality video tutorials directly within the app. This will allow us to leverage YouTube's robust video hosting capabilities and ensure smooth playback and accessibility for users.  

Node.js: For server-side development, we will use Node.js, allowing us to create scalable backend services that support the app's various functionalities. 

 

Azure Cosmos DB: For scalable and secure data storage, we will use Azure Cosmos DB, a globally distributed, multi-model database service. This will handle user data, course content, and other critical information efficiently. 

 

Azure Blob Storage: We will use Azure Blob Storage for storing and managing large amounts of unstructured data, such as video files and images. It provides scalable and secure storage solutions, ensuring quick and reliable access to media content. 

React Native: Our app will be developed using React Native to ensure a seamless and responsive user interface. React Native allows us to build cross-platform applications with a single codebase, providing a consistent experience on both iOS and Android devices. 

Firebase Authentication: Firebase Authentication will be used for secure and easy user authentication, allowing users to sign up and log in using their email, phone number, or social media accounts. This ensures a smooth and secure user onboarding process. 

Azure Authentication: Azure Active Directory will be used for secure and seamless user authentication. This allows users to sign in using various methods, such as their email or through organizational accounts, ensuring a secure onboarding process. It integrates with external identity providers and offers multi-factor authentication, enhancing the overall security and user experience. 

 

 

Ethical Considerations 

To protect user data, robust encryption and secure authentication methods are implemented, adhering to regulations such as GDPR and CCPA, as emphasized by the International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP) (iapp.org). The app is designed with accessibility features like screen reader compatibility and multilingual support, following the World Health Organization's (WHO) guidelines on digital accessibility (who.int). Accurate nutritional information is provided, based on evidence-based research from the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics to support public health and prevent misinformation (eatright.org). Security considerations will be assessed to ensure best practices are followed as recommended by organizations such as OWASP (owasp.org), NIST (nist.gov), and CISA (cisa.gov). 

 

Any user-generated content will be moderated and governed by clear terms of service. We will maintain transparency by disclosing content sources and avoiding biased or commercial influences. Robust data protection measures will safeguard user privacy, and we will comply with regulations like GDPR and CCPA. The app will be designed to be culturally inclusive and accessible to all users, promoting a positive and responsible learning environment. 

 

Information contained within the application and its courses that is not common knowledge will be linked to sites where more information can be found. Content that includes research by persons other than those in this project group will be cited on the page on which they are contained.  

 

According to copyright.gov, “a mere list of ingredients” is not protected by copyright law, however “a recipe that creatively explains or depicts how or why to perform a particular activity may be copyrightable”.  If a non-original recipe is used, the original author will be credited, and a link will be provided to their original recipe whenever possible. It is worth noting that many instances of the same recipe can be found on various sites across the web or handed down through generations. When this is the case, it will be noted so as not to assume credit of a non-original recipe. Any international foods or techniques will be credited to the nationality from which they came, and fusion or inspired dishes will be noted as such.  

 

It is reasonable to expect that a content creator (blog, YouTube, etc.) might expect to be paid if their video appears on our learning application due to the time and labor required to record and edit videos for online consumption. For this reason, we will create our own videos rather than look for pre-existing videos or clips that demonstrate a particular skill. 

 

In the interest of integrity and accessibility, we will not be providing affiliate links or running add-space. Pop-up ads can not only be irritating but can cause accessibility issues for people with dyslexia, low vision, visual processing difficulties, or neurodivergence. They can also be quite difficult for individuals using screen reader software. Brand names of ingredients and cooking tools will be avoided. If brand names must be mentioned (to assist with identification of an ingredient) more than one brand name should be stated whenever possible.

Lastly, we all commit to putting our best foot forward and giving maximum effort to each task assigned. These guidelines are designed to foster accountability, communication, and proactive planning, ensuring the team's collective success.
